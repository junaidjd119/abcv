Key Concepts for Bicep Peak & Thickness Exercises

This 22-minute biceps mass builder combines heavy compound curls with targeted isolation work. Starts with weighted chin-ups for pre-exhaustion, progresses to barbell curls for overall mass, incline dumbbell curls for long head emphasis. Preacher curls for peak contraction. Uses rest-pause. Slow eccentrics (3-4 seconds) to maximize mechanical tension — a direct application of progressive overload through increased time under load. Perfect for intermediate lifters looking to add serious arm size.

Workout Specifications

Duration22 minutes
Difficulty LevelIntermediate
Target MusclesBiceps Brachii, Brachialis, Brachioradialis
Exercises IncludedWeighted chin-ups, barbell curls, incline dumbbell curls, preacher curls
Recommended Sets4 sets per exercise
Rep Range6-12 reps (strength/hypertrophy mix)
Equipment NeededBarbell, dumbbells, preacher bench, chin-up bar
Rest Periods60-90 seconds between sets

Pro Training Tips (Progressive Overload Focus)

  • Keep elbows pinned to your sides throughout all curl variations to isolate biceps.
  • Squeeze the biceps hard at the top of each rep for a 1-2 second peak contraction.
  • Control the negatives (lowering phase) for 3-4 seconds to increase time under tension — a key progressive overload variable.
  • Avoid swinging the torso; if you need momentum. The weight is too heavy.
  • Apply overload by adding 2.5kg or 1 extra rep per set each week while keeping form.
  • Use rest-pause on the last set: after reaching failure, rest 15s. Continue for 2-3 more reps.

Key Concepts for Bicep Peak & Thickness Exercises

A 15-minute high-volume circuit using 4 biceps exercises performed back-to-back with minimal rest. Features cable curls, hammer curls, reverse curls, and concentration curls. Each station lasts 45 seconds with 15 seconds transition. This metabolic training increases sarcoplasmic muscle growth and builds muscular endurance. Progressive overload is achieved by increasing work duration (e.g.. 50s instead of 45s) or reducing rest (10s) week by week. Perfect for finishing arm day or as a standalone pump session.

Workout Specifications

Duration15 minutes
Difficulty LevelAll Levels
Target MusclesBiceps Brachii, Brachialis, Forearms
Exercises IncludedCable curls, hammer curls, reverse curls, concentration curls
Recommended Sets3-4 rounds of the circuit
Rep Range45 seconds per exercise (approx. 15-25 reps)
Equipment NeededCable machine, dumbbells, bench
Rest Periods15 seconds between exercises; 60s between rounds

Pro Training Tips

  • Minimize rest — transition quickly to keep the pump and metabolic stress.
  • Focus on the squeeze and mind-muscle connection rather than heavy weight.
  • Control tempo: 2 seconds up, 2 seconds down even during the circuit.
  • Stay hydrated; the pump can be intense.
  • To progressively overload. Increase work intervals to 50 seconds or add a 5th exercise.
  • Use lighter weights to avoid form breakdown under fatigue.
High Pump Circuit Endurance Metabolic
3

Key Concepts for Bicep Peak & Thickness Exercises

This 25-minute definition-focused routine uses multiple curl variations to hit the biceps from every angle. Features spider curls for short head, incline curls for long head,. Cross-body hammer curls for brachialis emphasis. Strict form with 3-second eccentrics creates micro-tears for definition and separation. Progressive overload is applied by gradually increasing the eccentric duration (from 3s to 5s). Adding slight weight once 15 reps are achieved with perfect form.

Workout Specifications

Duration25 minutes
Difficulty LevelIntermediate
Target MusclesBiceps Brachii (long & short head), Brachialis
Exercises IncludedSpider curls, incline dumbbell curls, cross-body hammer curls, bayesian cable curls
Recommended Sets3 sets per exercise
Rep Range10-15 reps
Equipment NeededDumbbells, preacher bench, incline bench, cable machine
Rest Periods60 seconds between sets

Pro Training Tips

  • Strict form is paramount — no swinging, no shoulder involvement.
  • Use a full range of motion: extend elbows fully at bottom. Curl until supination is complete.
  • Peak contraction: hold for 2 seconds at the top for each rep.
  • For progressive overload, increase time under tension (slower eccentrics) before adding weight.
  • Mind-muscle link: visualize the biceps shortening during each curl.
  • Finish each exercise with a drop set (reduce weight by 20%. Go to failure).

4

Key Concepts for Bicep Peak & Thickness Exercises

A 20-minute mix workout using EZ bar curls for overall mass and dumbbells for unilateral work. The EZ bar reduces wrist strain while allowing heavy loading. Dumbbell work addresses strength imbalances. Allows greater range of motion, especially in the peak contraction. Progressive overload is applied via double progression: first increase reps within the 8-12 range. Then add weight. Includes 21s (partial reps) as an effort technique on the final set.

Workout Specifications

Duration20 minutes
Difficulty LevelIntermediate
Target MusclesBiceps Brachii, Brachialis, Forearms
Exercises IncludedEZ bar curls, alternating dumbbell curls, seated dumbbell curls, hammer curls
Recommended Sets4 sets (2 EZ bar, 2 dumbbell)
Rep Range8-12 reps
Equipment NeededEZ curl bar, dumbbells, preacher bench (optional)
Rest Periods60-75 seconds between sets

Pro Training Tips

  • Use the EZ bar for heavier compound sets to protect wrists; keep grip shoulder-width.
  • With dumbbells. Supinate (rotate palms up) fully at the top to recruit both biceps heads.
  • Control the weight on the way down; don't let it drop.
  • To overload, use the "2-0-2" tempo. Aim to beat last week's reps on your last set.
  • Incorporate "21s": 7 partials from bottom to mid. 7 from mid to top, 7 full range — great for the final set.
  • Keep elbows stationary throughout; imagine they are pinned to your sides.
EZ Bar Dumbbell Combo Unilateral Strength & Hypertrophy
5

Key Concepts for Bicep Peak & Thickness Exercises

This 18-minute isolation session eliminates momentum and helper muscles. Features concentration curls, cable curls with back against wall. Machine preacher curls. The strict form ensures 100% biceps recruitment. Perfect for improving mind-muscle connection and carving peak. Progressive overload is applied by increasing the pause at peak contraction (up to 3 seconds). Using iso-holds at the most difficult angle. Ideal for those wanting to bring up lagging biceps.

Workout Specifications

Duration18 minutes
Difficulty LevelIntermediate
Target MusclesBiceps Brachii (peak focus)
Exercises IncludedConcentration curls, cable curls (back against wall), machine preacher curls, spider curls
Recommended Sets3 sets per exercise
Rep Range12-15 reps
Equipment NeededDumbbells, cable machine, preacher machine, bench
Rest Periods45-60 seconds between sets

Pro Training Tips

  • Eliminate all momentum — if you can't perform a rep without body english. Reduce weight.
  • Focus on the squeeze at the top; hold for 2-3 seconds to increase effort.
  • Use a slow tempo (3-1-3) to maximize isolation and time under tension.
  • Lighter weight is often more effective for isolation; prioritize feeling over moving load.
  • Apply progressive overload by extending the eccentric phase or adding an iso-hold at the hardest point.
  • Perform concentration curls seated, elbow pressed against inner thigh, to avoid cheating.
