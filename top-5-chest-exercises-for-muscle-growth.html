1

Key Concepts for Upper & Lower Chest Workout

A comprehensive 35-minute chest session covering all angles for complete development. Starts with heavy flat barbell press for overall mass (4 sets. 6-8 reps), moves to incline dumbbell press for upper chest (4x8-10), then decline press for lower pecs (3x10-12). Includes dumbbell flyes (3x12-15) for deep stretch. Cable crossovers (3x15-20) for peak contraction. Progressive overload is built in: increase weight on main lifts each week. Add reps on accessories, or reduce rest intervals. Perfect for intermediate lifters seeking balanced chest growth.

Workout Specifications

Duration35 minutes
Difficulty LevelIntermediate
Target MusclesPectoralis Major (sternal/clavicular), Anterior Delts, Triceps
Exercises IncludedFlat barbell press, incline dumbbell press, decline press, dumbbell flyes, cable crossovers
Recommended Sets4 sets per main lift, 3 sets for accessories
Rep Range6-20 (strength to hypertrophy)
Equipment NeededBarbell, dumbbells, flat/incline/decline bench, cable machine
Rest Periods60-90 sec for presses, 45-60 sec for flyes/crossovers

Pro Training Tips (Progressive Overload Focus)

  • Always warm up shoulders with band pull‑aparts. Light rotator cuff work before pressing.
  • Control the negative (lowering) phase for 2‑3 seconds to increase time under tension.
  • Use a full range of motion: touch bar to chest on presses. Stretch deeply on flyes.
  • Squeeze pectorals at the top of flyes. Crossovers for a peak contraction.
  • Apply overload by adding 2.5kg to barbell presses weekly while keeping rep targets.
  • For dumbbell work. Once you hit 12 reps on the final set, move up in weight next session.
  • Use a spotter on heavy sets to safely push beyond failure if desired.
Full Chest Mass Builder Progressive Overload Intermediate
2

Key Concepts for Upper & Lower Chest Workout

This 28‑minute advanced session employs techniques like rest‑pause, mechanical drop sets. Accommodating resistance to break plateaus. Learn to use chain presses (adds chains to barbell for variable resistance), board presses (limit range to overload lockout). Floor presses (triceps & lockout focus). Also features band‑resisted push‑ups and dumbbell pullovers with timed eccentric. Designed for experienced lifters who have stalled on traditional pressing.

Workout Specifications

Duration28 minutes
Difficulty LevelAdvanced
Target MusclesPectoralis Major (overall), Inner Chest, Triceps lockout
Exercises IncludedChain presses, board presses, floor presses, band‑resisted push‑ups, pullovers
Recommended Sets4 sets per technique
Rep Range3‑8 reps (strength focus)
Equipment NeededBarbell, chains, boards (2x4 or bench), resistance bands, floor space
Rest Periods2‑3 minutes for heavy work

Pro Training Tips

  • Use spotters or safety pins when training with chains/boards; setup is crucial.
  • Know your limits — these techniques are demanding; progress load gradually.
  • For board presses, start with 2‑board (less range). Gradually move to thinner boards.
  • Chain presses: add chains. That weight increases as you press up, overloading lockout.
  • Floor presses: keep elbows at 45° to protect shoulders; focus on triceps drive.
  • Apply progressive overload by adding small weight increases (1.25kg) or more chains.
Advanced Chains Board Press Accommodating Resistance
3

Key Concepts for Upper & Lower Chest Workout

A 25‑minute definition‑focused routine using lighter weights. Higher reps to etch detail and improve muscle separation. Exercises include cable crossovers (high‑to‑low, low‑to‑high for upper/lower chest), pec deck flyes, dumbbell pullovers (for serratus and rib cage expansion). Incline flyes. High‑rep sets (15‑25) create metabolic stress, sarcoplasmic muscle growth, and capillary density. Perfect for cutting phases or finishing chest day with a deep pump.

Workout Specifications

Duration25 minutes
Difficulty LevelIntermediate
Target MusclesInner Chest, Upper Chest, Serratus Anterior
Exercises IncludedCable crossovers (variations), pec deck, dumbbell pullovers, incline flyes
Recommended Sets4 sets per exercise
Rep Range15‑25 reps
Equipment NeededCable machine, pec deck, dumbbells, incline bench
Rest Periods45‑60 seconds

Pro Training Tips

  • Peak holds: at the point of peak contraction, pause for 2 seconds. Squeeze hard.
  • Control the tempo: use a 2‑1‑2 rhythm (2 sec eccentric. 1 sec hold, 2 sec concentric).
  • Light weight. Strict form — avoid using momentum; feel the inner chest working.
  • For progressive overload. Aim to increase total volume (sets × reps) or reduce rest by 5 seconds each week.
  • Mind‑muscle connection is critical; visualize the pectoral fibers shortening.
  • On pullovers, keep arms slightly bent. Focus on the stretch across the chest.
Sculpting High Reps Definition Inner Chest
4

Key Concepts for Upper & Lower Chest Workout

A 22‑minute power development session using explosive movements to recruit fast‑twitch fibers. Includes medicine ball chest passes (throw against wall or partner), plyometric push‑ups (clap or box), speed bench presses with light weight (50‑60% 1RM, explosive concentric),. Band‑resisted bench presses. The focus is on rate of force development. Ideal for athletes requiring upper‑body power (boxing, football, throwing sports). Rest periods are longer to keep neural freshness.

Workout Specifications

Duration22 minutes
Difficulty LevelAdvanced
Target MusclesPectoralis Major (fast‑twitch fibers), Triceps, Shoulder stabilizers
Exercises IncludedMedicine ball chest passes, plyo push‑ups, speed bench press, band‑resisted press
Recommended Sets5 sets per movement
Rep Range3‑5 explosive reps (ball passes: 6‑8 throws)
Equipment NeededMedicine ball (2‑5kg), light barbell, resistance bands, bench
Rest Periods2‑3 minutes to maintain power output

Pro Training Tips

  • Move explosively on every rep; the goal is speed, not fatigue.
  • Use light weight for speed bench (50‑60% 1RM). Focus on accelerating the bar.
  • For plyo push‑ups, land softly and immediately rebound into the next rep.
  • Full recovery between sets is key for power; don't rush.
  • Neural readiness: be fully focused before each set; power training requires CNS activation.
  • Progress by increasing the speed. Not the weight; once you can't be explosive, stop.
Power Explosive Fast‑Twitch Athletic
5

Key Concepts for Upper & Lower Chest Workout

A 30‑minute push‑day superset routine pairing chest. Triceps movements for efficiency and metabolic stress. Superset 1: Flat barbell press + close‑grip bench press (4×8‑12 each). Superset 2: Incline dumbbell flyes + overhead triceps extensions (3×12‑15). Superset 3: Parallel bar dips + triceps pushdowns (3×10‑15). The supersets save time while providing a powerful pump. Stimulating both muscle groups. Perfect for busy lifters who want a complete push workout in half an hour.

Workout Specifications

Duration30 minutes
Difficulty LevelIntermediate
Target MusclesPectoralis Major, Triceps Brachii, Anterior Delts
Exercises IncludedFlat barbell press, close‑grip bench, incline flyes, overhead extensions, dips, pushdowns
Recommended Sets4 supersets (3 exercises = 3 supersets)
Rep Range8‑15 reps per exercise
Equipment NeededBarbell, bench, dumbbells, cable machine, dip bars
Rest Periods60‑90 seconds between supersets

Pro Training Tips

  • Start with chest exercises first in each superset to prioritize them before triceps fatigue.
  • Minimize rest between exercises in a superset (15‑20 seconds) for metabolic effect.
  • Control the weight on flyes and extensions; avoid jerking.
  • For dips, lean forward slightly to emphasize chest, upright for triceps.
  • Apply overload by adding weight or reps on the first exercise of each superset each week.
  • Use a timer to keep supersets on track and rest steady.
